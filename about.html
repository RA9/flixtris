The Story of Flixtris

Flixtris began with a deep appreciation for the timeless brilliance of classic block-puzzle games. The simplicity, the pressure, and the quiet satisfaction of fitting pieces together inspired more than just nostalgia‚Äîit sparked a vision.

Built by me (Carlos S. Nah, alias RA9), someone who grew up loving puzzles and later fell in love with modern web technologies, Flixtris started as a small experiment. The question was simple: What if a classic game could feel modern, social, and accessible to everyone?

That experiment grew into a fully playable, browser-based multiplayer experience‚Äîcrafted line by line, tested relentlessly, and refined with care. Every feature reflects my belief that great games don't need bloated installs, paywalls, or barriers.

Our mission is simple: to create an engaging, accessible block-puzzle game that works everywhere, brings people together, and stays true to the joy that made us fall in love with gaming in the first place. Flixtris is open-source and community-driven‚Äîan open invitation to players and developers alike. Whether you're here to compete, relax, or contribute, you're part of the story.

Our Core Values

To keep the Flixtris community a fun and welcoming space for everyone, we ask our players and contributors to uphold these principles:

  • Inclusivity & Respect: We welcome players from all walks of life. We compete hard, but we remain kind. No hate speech, harassment, or bullying is tolerated.
  • Spirit of Play: We play for the joy of the game. Whether you win or lose, the goal is to enjoy the challenge and the flow.
  • Transparency & Openness: As an open-source project, we value honesty and collaboration. We grow by sharing ideas and helping one another improve.
  • Integrity: We celebrate skill and hard work. Dominating the leaderboard feels better when it's done through fair play and dedication.
  • Community First: Flixtris exists because of you. We listen to feedback and build features that make the experience better for the whole "Flixtrister" family.
